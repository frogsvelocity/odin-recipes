YAKISOBA

Takoyaki are a Japanese street snack that originated in the city of Osaka.
They are little round balls of batter that are slightly crispy on the outside and a bit soft and gooey on the inside,
stuffed with a little nugget of octopus, tempura bits, and green onions.

INGREDIENTS

¾ cup all-purpose flour
1 teaspoons baking powder
⅛ teaspoon kosher salt
1 egg
½ cup pre-steamed octopus, cut into bite-sized pieces
¼ cup tenkasu
2 tablespoons beni shoga (pickled red ginger), finely chopped
2 scallions, thinly sliced
apanese mayonnaise and takoyaki sauce, for serving
Katsuobushi and aonori for garnishing

DIRECTIONS

STEP 1

To make the takoyaki batter, combine the flour, baking powder, and salt in a large bowl.
Add the eggs, soy sauce, and dashi, and whisk to combine. Set aside.

STEP 2

Heat the takoyaki pan over medium-high heat. Thoroughly coat the wells with vegetable oil.
When the oil has begun to smoke a bit, and the pan is thoroughly heated, fill the wells with batter.
Top with a piece or two of octopus, tempura, ginger, and scallions, and cook until the bottoms of the balls are beginning to crisp.

STEP 3

Using chopsticks or wooden skewers, separate the layer of batter on top.
Rotate each takoyaki as they cook to give them a spherical shape.
Keep cooking for another 4 to 5 minutes, turning the takoyaki intermittently to get an even, golden brown exterior.

STEP 4

To serve, drizzle with Japanese mayonnaise, takoyaki sauce, and toppings of choice.
